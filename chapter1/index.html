Chapter I: Blood was dripping from his hands

The Blood was dripping from his hands.

His eyes were cold. In front of him, a myriad of body parts from something -large monstres you can tell- scattered. Usually, you don’t see such coldness in front of this shocking scene.

But again, his eyes were cold, as cold as ice, as if they could freeze you or devour you.

Back to then, a man, appearing impotent, was walking on the dust bowl of desert. He appeared so impotent as to be signified ironically, for you’d wondered how he survived in this dry, cold and dangerous land.

This land is famous for many kinds of monstres and beasts. Cruel as hell, some bartenders rediculed.

While the walking, the earth trembled, and the cracks widened. In lightening speed, a tentacle of unknown monstre attacked from the darkness of the cracks, along with dark and evil energy, unto this poor and impotent guy.

“He was too weak to survive.” I thought. He even wasn’t well armed with regular sets and cloths as everybody else. A fat weak merchant civilian can look 100 times more robust than he! How can this impotent guy survive?

If you were there, I doubt you’d open the eyes. But I did. To my surprise, this guy wasn’t tored apart imediately. What? He disappeared in my sight for a while.

“Hmm..mm..mm..” I only heared this mumbling.

The cracks widened even worse, and they are burning with white and green glimmers.

In fact, white and green winds blew everywhere during that moment.

Then I saw a cut -if not blew and blasted- tentacle painfully bleeding in dark colour. That guy showed up, and was in the kicking pose, idle. It appeared that this impotent guy knew how to kick, and the kick burned and blasted the huge tentacle like fairytells.

Suddenly, I couldn’t believe what I saw. Then he looked at me.

I knew this guy. His name is Rudi.

He said “my body is my weapon, so does my mind” unto me many years ago. I thought that was a joke. Now I was the witness of this joke.

Then he said, “how art thou?”

“How are you? That’s dangerous by the way… oh no!”

Sudden a monstruous claw attacked him from the underneath, that he couldn’t dodge or parry.

The monstre must still be alive, and very angry, revengeful, after the hazard.

The claw was so big, full of blades, and the blades were in the flesh and bone of Rudi, everywhere in his body.

Then Rudi bended over, gripping the claw with his arms and chest, a vertical “yoga” movement.

He spinned his waist -yellow glow this time- then the claw blasted, tore apart -a huge cracking and loud bang!

Now the monstre fully presented in the air, indeed with a cut tentacle and a cut claw. Only pain, revenge and rage are in its face -if you’d call such thing as a face.

“Hmm” Rudi mumbled. In fact, I wondered if I actually heard that sound. Or it was I imagined. I could hardly read what was like- perhaps “have..at..thee” in mumbling?

After the mumbling, several kicks and fists landed upon this large monstre. I wondered if the kicks were blades, and the fists were spears, because they created a foggy mist, a foggy mist of monstre’s dark blood, and body parts flew everywhere.

What were the shinbone and fingerbones made of? I thought bones were fragile… at least in my body. Let alone Rudi looks so weak and impotent.

Then he said “farewell my friend”

Before he left, his blood was still dripping from his countless wounds. He appeared more than calm. “You don’t want to feel my pain, for you can bear it not. “ I recalled what he said several years ago. Maybe this is so-called “staggering” of the damage for a monk.

Then I never saw him again.
